Section 02

Investment Thesis

A fundamentally healthy business obscured by demerger mechanics. The balance sheet, not the business, drove the discount.

1. Demerger debt load
Resolved
GSK and Pfizer loaded Haleon with 4.0x ND/EBITDA at listing to extract value pre-demerger. The business itself was generating £1.5bn+ FCF from day one. The market penalised the equity for the balance sheet, not the fundamentals.
Now 2.6x. Target ~1.8x by FY2027E. Funded entirely from operating cash flow: £1.9bn FCF in FY2025, no asset sales, no equity issuance. Investment grade BBB+/A3. At 1.8x the balance sheet headwind is essentially gone.
2. GSK / Pfizer seller overhang
Resolved
Combined ~45% stake (~£14bn) held by motivated sellers at listing. Created persistent mechanical supply overhang: price discovery was impaired regardless of business performance or brand fundamentals.
Both have largely exited. Float now trades on fundamental merit. Price discovery is clean for the first time since listing.
3. Zantac litigation tail risk
Resolved
Potential unquantifiable liability as former GSK subsidiary. RBC Capital cited as primary headwind. Market applied a blanket discount to a business with no direct exposure.
US courts dismissed Zantac claims. GSK indemnification covers all pre-demerger exposure. Risk fully de-recognised. Haleon carries no residual liability.
The business was healthy throughout: 64% gross margins, £1.9bn FCF, category leadership in five segments. All three overhangs were balance sheet or technical, not operational. With each now resolved, valuation reflects current earnings. Applying peer median 13.7x to FY2027E EBITDA (£3,312m) implies 456p (+25%). No re-rating required.

Earnings Growth Drivers

Why EBITDA grows from £2,838m (FY2025A) to £3,312m (FY2027E)

FY2025A: £2,838m
→

1. Margin expansion
Primary driver
  • Cost of sales fell £605m in FY2023–2025 while revenue fell only £272m. Costs declined 2x faster than revenue. Already visible in reported financials.
  • Gross margin expanded ~350bps in FY2025 alone (59.7% to 64.2%). Adj. EBITDA margin: 25.7% in FY2025A, guided toward 28–30% by FY2030E.
  • £800m supply chain programme tracking ahead of plan. SKU rationalisation, logistics optimisation, manufacturing consolidation. Structural, not cyclical.
2. Oral Health quality supports margin
  • Oral Health (32% of group revenue) grew 7.9% organically in FY2025, above group average. Highest-margin segment in the portfolio.
  • Despite private label pressure, Sensodyne increased penetration by ~130bps in FY2025, indicating sustained consumer preference and pricing resilience.
  • As Oral Health grows as a share of mix, blended group margin expands mechanically. No new assumptions required.
3. Growth normalisation
  • FY2025 organic growth of 3% reflected temporary factors: weak US cold/flu season (Respiratory -1.9%) and VMS post-pandemic normalisation (VMS +1.9% vs historical +5–8%).
  • Pre-results consensus stood at 4.4% for FY2026; management guided below, sending shares 6% lower. CEO confirmed FY2027 recovery to 4–6% (Feb 2026).
  • Respiratory and VMS both carry easier comparatives in FY2026. Neither impairment was structural.
4. Mix and geography
  • APAC delivered +5.2% organic in FY2025, now ~23% of revenue. China recovering from post-COVID destocking; India growing double digits. Structural tailwind, not one-off.
  • Emerging markets growing faster than legacy developed markets. Higher-growth, higher-margin categories (Oral Health, OTC) increasing as share of mix.
  • North America organic -0.4% in FY2025; recovery supported by Sensodyne distribution resets and VMS comp normalisation in H2 FY2026.
Takeaway: EBITDA growth from £2,838m to £3,312m is driven primarily by margin expansion already visible in reported financials, supported by cyclical growth normalisation. No heroic assumptions required.

Six material risks identified — none fatal to the thesis

Leverage; refinancing risk LOW Net debt £7.3bn at 2.6x. Rising rates increase refinancing cost on £836m short-term debt. £1.9bn FCF covers £600m paydown + £357m interest with headroom. BBB+/A3 investment grade.

Private label / competitive pressure LOW OTC private label gains share in consumer stress environments. Clinical efficacy protects Sensodyne: 130bps penetration increase FY2025 despite private label growth.
Regulatory / product liability MED OTC reclassification risk; residual pre-demerger GSK claims. GSK indemnification covers pre-demerger claims. Diversified portfolio (5 categories, 170+ markets).
Multiple compression / sector rotation LOW Structural risk: Haleon may remain anchored to mid-tier OTC peers if growth and margin expansion fail to converge toward premium consumer health benchmarks. OTC healthcare is non-discretionary. Dividend yield provides floor. LBO floor at 474p limits downside.
